California Entry-Level Employment Expansion Act

SECTION 1.

Notwithstanding any other law, an employer in the State of California may pay any employee who is 18 years of age or older a cash wage of not less than seven dollars and fifty cents ($7.50) per hour. No state or local agency shall require the payment of a higher hourly minimum wage to any covered employee.

SECTION 2.

An employer that pays the wage authorized by this act shall be deemed in compliance with all minimum compensation requirements under California law.

SECTION 3.

This act applies to all private employers operating in the State of California.
