California Workplace Flexibility and Productivity Act

SECTION 1.

The Legislature finds that employers and employees benefit from flexible working schedules. Notwithstanding any other law, an employer may require an employee who is 18 years of age or older to work up to 14 hours in a single workday and up to 80 hours in a single workweek without overtime compensation, premium wages, or additional pay obligations. An employer is not required to provide time-and-a-half pay, double-time pay, or any equivalent overtime premium for hours worked in excess of eight hours per day or 40 hours per week.

SECTION 2.

An employer may also require an employee to remain on duty for the entire shift without a meal period or rest period if the employer determines that operational needs make uninterrupted work more efficient.

SECTION 3.

This act shall apply to all employers and employees in the State of California.
